As of writting I have restored three seperate IBM keyboard. Two of which are propper Model M keyboards, one blue badge and one grey oval badge. The other keyboard is an M122 Battlecruiser. The M122 is the first IBM keyboard I ever found, used, and have restored. Found in the dusty server room equiptment locker of my employer at the time I found it discarded in a pile of unused parts. My M122 was once used as the terminal access keyobard of the AS/400 mainframe that the company used as its database. As a quick side note the company still uses an AS/400 running the exact same data base managemnt software as of writting in July 2023, albeiet thorugh an emulator.

When I found my M122 it was not in good shape, and I asked my boss if I could have it since it clearly hasn't been used in years. He agreed and I begen to research how to restore such a keyboard. The first step was a cleaning process, since it was abolutly disgusting when I found it.
